# MEG : Before starting the acquisition

### Loading preparation on MEG computer

1)      You first have to **load your project**:

* Settings _→_ Change Project _→_Project: YOUR_PROJECT
* Change the Subject to: Subject Subject
* Click File → Load Settings → std  Most people call their settings "std" but actually you can call them how you like as long as you find them again. Click _Save_ and the window will close.

2)      Then you have to **load the exact preparation file** that you saved on the preparation computer: (Note: look for exact date and time – sometimes there are several files with similar time)

* Load the preparation file: _File → Load preparation → projectname+time_

### Taking the participant into the room

1. Plug in **all electrodes**BC1(+/-): EOG 001 = EOG VBC2(+/-): EOG 002 = EOG HBC3(+/-): ECG 003
1. Plug in the **HPI coils** into the red plug
1. In case of an **auditory experiment**: Provide **the inner ear phones** (check that left is left and right is right) and instruct how to insert them.
1. Provide the **response box** and explain how to use it
1. Push the participant up until he can **feel the helmet** with the **top** of his head
1. Attach the **chin rest firmly** to prevent head movement
1. Place the **monitor in the correct position** (see signs on the floor)

### Talking to the participant

There are two possibilities to talk to the participant while he is in the MEG:

1. Talking via the **Intercom** (when the person does NOT have in-ear headphones on):

* There are different modes:**10** – default mode: the Intercom is switched off
* If you wish to talk to the participant, to the following:**press 11**: now the participant can hear you and you can hear the participant**press T long**: now you can hear the participant, but the participant cannot hear you**press T again short:** now the participant can hear you again**press X:** now the Intercom is switched off

1. Talking via the **microphone** (when the person does have in-ear headphones on):

* To talk to the participant, press the **green button** VERY FIRM!

Sometimes there are problems with the microphone and the participant cannot hear you properly. Talk to Fred. he has some magic fixing skills - but it will take some time (not a-few-seconds-job).

### Instructions before data acquisition

* remind participants that they **should not move** because head needs to stay in the same position for the whole measurement (MEG channels fix, so head needs to stay fix to measure brain activity from same spots)
* **remind them after every block** that they should not move as muscle activity contaminates our data /brainactivity of interest and analysis becomes more challenging
* provide experiment-specific feedback

### Starting acquisition and saving data

#### Subject Code

**Birth date + 1st and 3rd letters of mothers name and maiden name: YYYYMMDDXXYY**

e.g.: Max Mustermann, 25.10.1990, Mother: Mona Müller = 19901025MNML

#### Starting the acquisition

1. Click _GO!_ on [this window](attachments/27407813/27407818.png)
1. The computer will ask you to measure the position of the HPI coils. Click _Measure_ and normally a window will appear, where you can click **Accept**.**NOTE:** Before you click _Measure_, make sure to tell the participant againt that it is VERY IMPORTANT to keep the head still from now on!
1. Now you can click _Record Raw_ and – if required – cHPI (continious HPI).

### Saving the data

When you want to save the data, you just click _Stop_.

Then [this window](attachments/27407813/27407819.png) will appear.

The most important thing is that your **file always starts with the subject code** you generated before. Then you can name your data how you like.

**Best practice is: SUBJECTCODE_blockXX.**

### Resting state measurement

Before starting the data collection for the experiment, everybody is advised to do a resting state measurement for about 5 minutes with the MEG door closed!

Make sure to present a fixation cross on the screen that participants can fixate during the recording (eyes open!). You will save the data in the same folder as in your project! File name should be: **subj_id**_rest
