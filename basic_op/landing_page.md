# **Basic Operating Procedures**
---
Hi and welcome to the lab! This tutorial is designed to guide you smoothly through the onboarding process and provide you with all the essentials to get started in the lab. You’ll need your work laptop and user account to begin.

- If you haven’t received your laptop or user-account (i.e., the b123456 + passwort) yet, please contact [Sabine Stummvoll](https://www.plus.ac.at/psychologie/studium/anlaufstellen/stummvoll-sabine/).


The tutorial is divided into four parts: <br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;**1. [Initial Setup](initial_setup.md):** Get to know the lab’s communication channels and learn how to obtain your keys and copy card.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;**2. [Laptop Setup](laptop_setup.md):** Set up your laptop with the standard programs we use across the lab to ensure a consistent workflow.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;**3. [Coding Workflow and Cluster Computing](coding_cluster.md):** Programs to download for version control and code management, and for working with the cluster.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;**4. [Data Management](data_management.md):** Information about where MEG, MRI, and eye-tracking data are stored.

Additionally, you can find links to helpful online tutorials in all sections and links to lab resources and tutorials. If you click on the headings, you can directly download the respective programs or access other relevant pages. Enjoy!

### Further Resources
The [PLUS-intranet](https://im.sbg.ac.at/) is a very useful hub provided by the University of Salzburg and contains tutorials, guides, and FAQs on topics such as email, data storage, printing, network access, and more. Additionally, Thomas Hartmann wrote a bunch of very helpful tutorials specifically tailored to the lab's operating procedures including tutorials on [using Python for science](https://thht.gitlab.io/tutorials/index.html#troubleshooting-and-faqs), setting up and using [Git and Gitlab](https://thht.gitlab.io/tutorials/using_python_for_science/01_prepare_computer_for_scientific_python/git.html), and on [troubleshooting and FAQs](https://thht.gitlab.io/tutorials/index.html#troubleshooting-and-faqs). *(Please note here that as these tutorials were written a while ago, they still talk about 'bombers', which were the remote machines the lab used before the cluster was introduced. You can ignore that. Otherwise, they are up-to-date.)* We highly recommend reading there if you have any further questions.
